Jollof Rice

Back to Home Page

Description:

Jollof rice is a West African dish that is a staple food in most households.Jollof rice is a much-loved festive dish that is often served at weddings, parties, and other large gatherings.
It is a brightly colored orange to red rice dish, due to being cooked in a rich and spicy tomato-based sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 4 Tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper
  • 1 Tablespoon tomato puree
  • 6 large, fresh tomatoes or 1 and ½ cans of tomato, chopped
  • 1 seasoning cube/bouillon cube
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 2 cups long grain rice
  • 2 cups hot water or stock/broth
  • ½ cup mixed carrots and peas
  • A round of parchment paper that fits inside your cooking pot

Steps:

  1. Pour oil into large saucepan. Cook onion in oil over medium-low heat until translucent.
  2. Stir in stewed tomatoes and tomato paste, and season with salt, black pepper, cayenne pepper, red pepper flakes, Worcestershire sauce and rosemary. Cover, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, stir in water, and add chicken pieces. Simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Stir in rice, carrots, and green beans, and season with nutmeg. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover, and simmer until the chicken is fork-tender and the rice is cooked, 25 to 30 minutes.
